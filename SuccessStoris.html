Success Stories

Abu Ammar Coffee

In the West Bank, 1998, founded by a Palestinian family from Ramallah, Abu Ammar Coffee started as a small café offering traditional Arabic coffee and some local pastries. The idea was to create a space that brought the local community together and celebrated Palestinian heritage through coffee and food.

In its early days, Abu Ammar Coffee faced many challenges, particularly due to the difficult political and economic conditions in the region. The security situation affected the flow of customers, making it difficult to maintain the café's operations. Instead of giving in to these challenges, the family focused on providing a unique experience for their customers. They expanded the menu to include traditional dishes like falafel, hummus, and kibbeh, which helped attract more people. They also organized cultural and musical events and added a variety of specialty coffees, further enhancing the café’s appeal. The café's location, with its warm and inviting atmosphere, played a significant role in improving the quality of the coffee and contributed to making it a cultural center, rather than just a café.

Over time, Abu Ammar Coffee began attracting customers from different regions. It gained a reputation for its high-quality coffee and distinctive flavors, and successfully built a large and loyal customer base. The owners expanded the café and opened new branches in other cities, which helped to further strengthen the brand.

Today, Abu Ammar Coffee is one of the most popular cafés in Palestine. It has gained widespread fame for the quality of its beverages and food, in addition to the warm and comfortable atmosphere it provides. The café is also committed to working with local farmers to offer fresh, organic ingredients. Our location is not just about coffee; it's about enjoying an experience that is crafted by Palestinian hands, with every cup and every dish telling the story of our culture and heritage.

Za'atar Coffee

Hala Saleem, the Lebanese entrepreneur, has had a deep passion for coffee since she was young, always in search of a new and unique flavor for her favorite drink. Amid the challenging circumstances following Lebanon's economic crisis, Hala faced significant hurdles, including unemployment and a lack of opportunities. However, she decided to find a way to combine her love for coffee with an innovative idea to carve a path forward.

Hala came up with the idea of blending coffee with za'atar, a popular ingredient in Lebanese cuisine that adds a distinctive flavor to many dishes. She began experimenting at home, using simple ingredients and recipes of her own creation. However, her initial attempts did not turn out as expected, as she struggled to find the right balance of flavors. Despite the setbacks, she did not give up and kept refining her approach until she successfully developed the perfect blend.

During her experiments, Hala discovered an app called "Coffee Academy," which provided valuable tips on improving coffee flavor and refining her blends. The app helped her hone her roasting skills and innovate further, contributing to the improvement of her final product.

Once Hala perfected her coffee-za'atar blend, she decided to turn her passion into a business. However, the road was not easy. She faced difficulties in finding an affordable location and securing the necessary materials due to the ongoing economic crisis. Despite these challenges, she opened the first branch of "Za'atar Coffee" in a modest neighborhood in Beirut.

Qahwa Nuzul

"Qahwa Nuzul" was founded in 2006 in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, by a group of young Saudis who had a deep passion for coffee and saw it as an opportunity to achieve their dream. They didn’t have a large capital or huge investments, but they relied on their love for the product and their desire to offer a unique coffee experience to the people of Saudi Arabia.

The first challenge they faced was finding a suitable location to showcase their products. At the time, the local market was saturated with large Western coffee chains like Starbucks and Café Coffee Day. However, they didn’t let that stop them. They started with a small café in a popular neighborhood, offering high-quality coffee with an authentic Arabic touch.

Over time, "Qahwa Nuzul" gained popularity among Saudis, who felt a sense of connection with the brand’s identity. The cafés started spreading to different cities in the kingdom, but they continued to face various challenges, such as managing quality across all branches and ensuring smooth operational processes. They focused on training staff and providing the best possible service. This became a key factor in their success, as they realized that coffee wasn’t just a drink; it was a social experience.
