American Coffee




Equipment Used:


  • Manual coffee vessel (like a French press or pour-over tool).
  • Coffee machine (like a drip coffee maker).

Type of Coffee Beans Used:


  • Arabica beans (light or medium roast beans can be used).

Coffee Ratio:


  • 1:15 (15 grams of coffee per 225 ml of water).

Water Ratio:


  • 225 ml per cup.

Milk Ratio:


  • Optional (milk can be added as desired).


Hot American Coffee Preparation


Manual Method:


  1. Boil Water:Heat water to about 90–96°C (195–205°F).

  2. Prepare Coffee: Grind 15 grams of coffee beans to a medium consistency.

  3. Add Coffee to Vessel: Place the ground coffee in the manual coffee vessel.

  4. Add Water: Pour 225 ml of hot water over the coffee.

  5. Stir: Gently stir the coffee and water to ensure they mix.

  6. Wait: Let the coffee steep for 4 minutes.

  7. Press and Filter: If using a French press, slowly press down the plunger.

  8. Serve and Enjoy: Serve the hot coffee in a large cup, and add milk or cream if desired.


Machine Method:


  1. Prepare Machine: Ensure the machine is clean and heated.

  2. Prepare Coffee: Grind 15 grams of coffee beans to a medium consistency.

  3. Add Coffee to Filter: Place the ground coffee in the machine's filter.

  4. Add Water: Pour 225 ml of hot water over the coffee.

  5. Brew Coffee: Allow the coffee to brew into the carafe or cup.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Serve the hot coffee in a large cup, and add milk or cream if desired.



Cold American Coffee Preparation


Manual Method:


  1. Prepare Coffee: Grind 15 grams of coffee beans to a medium consistency.

  2. Add Coffee to Vessel: Place the ground coffee in the manual coffee vessel.

  3. Add Cold Water: Pour 225 ml of cold water over the coffee.

  4. Stir: Gently stir the coffee and water to ensure they mix.

  5. Wait: Let the coffee steep for 12–24 hours in the refrigerator.

  6. Filter: After steeping, filter the coffee using a strainer or filter.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Serve the coffee over ice in a glass, and add milk or cream if desired.


Machine Method:


  1. Prepare Coffee: Grind 15 grams of coffee beans to a medium consistency.

  2. Add Coffee to Filter: Place the ground coffee in the machine's filter.

  3. Add Cold Water: Pour 225 ml of cold water into the machine.

  4. Brew Coffee: Allow the coffee to brew into the carafe or cup.

  5. Serve and Enjoy: Serve the coffee over ice in a glass, and add milk or cream if desired.

Americano




Equipment Used:


  • Espresso Machine.

Type of Coffee Beans Used:


  • Arabica beans (can use medium or dark roasted beans).

Coffee Ratio:


  • 1:2 (one shot of espresso for every 60 ml of water).

Water Ratio:


  • 60 ml of hot water for each shot of espresso.

Milk Ratio:


  • Optional (milk can be added to taste).


Hot Americano Preparation



Preparation Steps:



  1. Prepare the Espresso:

    • Grind 18-20 grams of coffee beans to a suitable fine consistency.

    • Place the ground coffee in the filter and tamp it down gently.

    • Close the filter in the espresso machine and start brewing to extract a single or double shot of espresso (about 30-60 ml).

  2. Heat the Water:

    Boil water at around 90–96°C (195–205°F).

  3. Combine Espresso and Water:

    • In a cup, pour 60 ml of hot water.

    • Add the brewed espresso to the hot water.

  4. Serve and Enjoy:

    Stir gently if desired, and enjoy your hot Americano. You can add milk or cream if you wish.


Cold Americano Preparation


Preparation Steps:


  1. Prepare the Espresso:

    • Grind 18-20 grams of coffee beans to a suitable fine consistency.

    • Place the ground coffee in the filter and tamp it down gently.

    • Close the filter in the espresso machine and start brewing to extract a single or double shot of espresso (about 30-60 ml).

  2. Prepare the Ice:

    Fill a cup with ice cubes.

  3. Combine Espresso and Cold Water:

    • Pour 60 ml of cold water over the ice in the cup.

    • Add the brewed espresso to the cold water.

  4. Serve and Enjoy:

    Stir gently if desired, and enjoy your refreshing cold Americano. You can add milk or cream if you wish.

Cold Brew




Equipment Used:


  • Large coffee container or pitcher.
  • Coffee filter (such as cloth or paper filter).
  • Coffee grinder (for coarse grinding).

Type of Coffee Beans Used:


  • Arabica beans (light or medium roast is preferred).

Coffee Ratio:


  • 1:4 (100 grams of coffee for every 400 ml of water).

Water Ratio:


  • 400 ml of water for every 100 grams of coffee.

Milk Ratio:


  • Optional (milk can be added to taste).


Cold Brew Preparation


Preparation Steps:



  1. Grind the Coffee:

    • Grind 100 grams of coffee beans to a coarse consistency, larger than salt grains.

  2. Mix Coffee and Water:
    • In a large container or pitcher, add the ground coffee.

    • Pour 400 ml of cold water over the coffee.

  3. Stir:

    Gently stir the mixture to ensure all coffee grounds are saturated.

  4. Steep the Coffee:

    • Cover the container or pitcher and let it steep in the refrigerator for 12-24 hours. The longer it steeps, the stronger the flavor.

  5. Filter the Coffee:

    • After steeping, use a coffee filter to strain the coffee from the grounds.

    • You can use a cloth filter or a fine mesh strainer.

  6. Serve:

    • Serve the cold brew in a cup filled with ice.

    • You can add water or milk to taste.

  7. Enjoy:

    Stir gently if desired, and enjoy your refreshing cold brew coffee.

V60 Coffee




Equipment Used:


  • V60 Dripper

Type of Coffee Beans Used:


  • Medium roast Arabica beans (or specify any other preferred type, like light roast or dark roast)

Coffee Ratio:


  • 1:15 (15 grams of coffee per 225 ml water)

Water Ratio:


  • 225 ml for one cup

Milk Ratio:


  • None

Flavor Type:


  • Pure coffee flavor, no added flavors


Hot V60 Coffee


Preparation Steps:


  1. Boil the Water:

    • Heat filtered water to around 90–96°C (195–205°F), just below boiling.

    • While the water heats, measure about 15-20 grams of coffee for a single cup (adjust according to taste).

  2. Prepare the Filter:

    • Place the paper filter in the V60 dripper.

    • Pour hot water over the filter to rinse it and warm up the dripper, which also reduces any paper taste. Discard the rinsing water.

  3. Add Ground Coffee:

    • Place the V60 on your cup or server, and add the ground coffee into the filter.

    • Gently shake or tap the V60 to level the coffee bed for even extraction.

  4. Bloom:

    • Start a timer. Pour a small amount of hot water (about 30–40 ml) over the coffee grounds, just enough to saturate them.

    • Let the coffee bloom for 30–45 seconds, releasing carbon dioxide and enhancing flavor.

  5. Begin the Main Pour:

    • After the bloom, slowly pour water in a circular motion over the coffee, starting from the center and moving outward.

    • Pour steadily until you reach the target water amount (200 ml for a single cup), maintaining a slow and steady stream.

  6. Allow Coffee to Drain:

    • Once you reach your water target, allow the coffee to drain completely. The entire brewing process should take around 2:30 to 3 minutes.

  7. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Remove the V60 dripper, give the brewed coffee a gentle swirl, and pour it into your cup if using a server. Enjoy the clean, aromatic flavors of your V60 coffee!



Cold V60 Coffee


Preparation Steps:


  1. Prepare the Ice:

    • Place about 100-150 grams of ice cubes in your cup or server, filling it about halfway.

  2. Prepare the Filter:

    • Place the paper filter in the V60 dripper.

  3. Add Ground Coffee:

    • Measure 15-20 grams of coffee and add it to the filter.

    • Gently shake the V60 to level the coffee bed.

  4. Bloom:

    • Pour a small amount of cold water (about 30–40 ml) over the coffee grounds, just enough to saturate them.

    • Let the coffee bloom for 30–45 seconds.

  5. Begin the Main Pour:

    • Slowly pour the remaining cold water in a circular motion over the coffee, starting from the center and moving outward.

    • Ensure that all the water is poured over the coffee grounds to extract the flavors.

  6. Allow Coffee to Drain:

    • Let the coffee drain completely into the cup or server. This may take a little longer than hot brewing.

  7. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Stir the brewed coffee if desired, and enjoy your refreshing cold V60 coffee!

Arabic Coffee




Types of Arabic Coffee:


  • Black Arabic Coffee: Black Arabic coffee is a traditional beverage in Arab cultures and is often served during social occasions.

  • Yellow Arabic Coffee: This is a different type of coffee that is usually prepared with added spices such as cardamom, giving it a distinctive flavor and a light yellow color.



Preparation of Black Arabic Coffee


Ingredients:

  • Coffee Beans: Roasted Arabic coffee beans.

  • Water: Pure water.

Coffee to Water Ratio:

  • The typical ratio is 1:10 (10 grams of coffee for every 100 ml of water).

Preparation Method:

  1. Prepare the Coffee:

    • Grind 10-15 grams of Arabic coffee beans until fine.

  2. Boil the Water:

    • Bring water to a boil in a pot.

  3. Add the Coffee:

    • Add the ground coffee to the boiling water.

  4. Cooking:

    • Let the coffee simmer on low heat for 10-15 minutes.

  5. Serving:

    • Pour the coffee into small cups and serve hot.



Preparation of Yellow Arabic Coffee


Ingredients:

  • Coffee Beans: Roasted Arabic coffee beans.

  • Water: Pure water.

  • Spices: Ground cardamom or cinnamon as desired.

Coffee to Water Ratio:

  • The typical ratio is 1:10 (10 grams of coffee for every 100 ml of water).

Preparation Method:

  1. Prepare the Coffee:

    • Grind 10-15 grams of Arabic coffee beans until fine.

  2. Boil the Water:

    • Bring water to a boil in a pot.

  3. Add Coffee and Spices:

    • Add the ground coffee and preferred spices (like cardamom) to the boiling water.

  4. Cooking:

    • Let the coffee simmer on low heat for 10-15 minutes.

  5. Serving:

    • Pour the coffee into small cups and serve hot.


Tips:

  • Spices: The amount of spices can be adjusted according to taste for a distinctive flavor.

  • Serving: Arabic coffee is best served in small cups alongside dates or traditional sweets.

Cortado




Equipment Used:


  • Espresso machine.
  • Double or small serving cup.
  • Coffee grinder.

Type of Coffee Beans Used:


  • Arabica beans (light or medium roast is preferred).

Coffee Ratio:


  • 1:1 (one shot of espresso for every equal amount of steamed milk).

Water Ratio:


  • 30-60 ml of water to prepare the espresso.

Milk Ratio:


  • 30-60 ml of steamed milk (depending on the desired size).


Hot Cortado Preparation:


Preparation Steps:


  1. Prepare the Espresso:

    • Grind 18-20 grams of coffee beans to a suitable consistency for espresso.

    • Place the ground coffee in the filter and tamp it down gently.

    • Close the filter in the espresso machine and start brewing to extract a single or double shot of espresso (about 30-60 ml).

  2. Steam the Milk:

    • While preparing the espresso, steam 30-60 ml of milk using a milk steamer or espresso machine until it becomes creamy.

  3. Combine Espresso and Milk:

    • In a serving cup, pour the brewed espresso.

    • Add the steamed milk over the espresso.

  4. Serve and Enjoy:

    • You can garnish the surface with a sprinkle of cinnamon or cocoa if desired.

    • Enjoy your rich and flavorful hot cortado!

Flat White




Ingredients:


  • Coffee Beans: Arabica beans are preferred, and you can use light or medium roast beans.

  • Water: About 30-60 ml of water is used to prepare the espresso.

  • Milk: A flat white requires approximately 120-150 ml of steamed milk.


Coffee to Milk Ratio:


  • The typical ratio of espresso to milk is about 1:4, giving the drink a strong flavor with creamy milk.


Hot Flat White Preparation:


Preparation Steps:


  1. Prepare the Espresso:

    • Grind 18-20 grams of coffee beans to a suitable fineness.

    • Place the coffee in the espresso machine filter and tamp it gently.

    • Brew a single or double shot of espresso (about 30-60 ml).

  2. Steam the Milk:

    • Use a milk steamer to steam 120-150 ml of milk until creamy with light froth.

  3. Combine Espresso and Milk:

    • In a serving cup, pour the prepared espresso.

    • Slowly add the steamed milk over the espresso to create a smooth layer of milk on top.

  4. Serving:

    • It’s preferred to serve the flat white in glass or ceramic cups to enjoy its beautiful appearance.



Iced Flat White Preparation:


Preparation Steps:


  1. Prepare the Espresso:

    • Grind 18-20 grams of coffee beans as mentioned above.

    • Brew a shot of espresso.

  2. Prepare the Ice:

    • Place an appropriate amount of ice in a serving cup.

  3. Chill the Milk:

    • Chill 120-150 ml of milk in the refrigerator.

  4. Combine Espresso and Milk:

    • Pour the prepared espresso over the ice.

    • Add the cold milk over the espresso.

  5. Serving:

    • It’s preferred to serve the iced flat white in a glass cup.

    • You can add a sprinkle of cocoa or cinnamon if desired.



Tips:


  • Froth: To achieve smooth froth, ensure not to overheat the milk. The temperature should be between 60-70°C (140-158°F).

  • Serving: It’s preferred to serve flat white hot or cold in small cups to enjoy the rich flavor.


Latte




Ingredients:


  • Coffee Beans: Arabica beans are preferred, either light or medium roast for a distinctive flavor.

  • Water: Use 30-60 ml of water to prepare the espresso.

  • Milk: A latte requires 90-120 ml of steamed milk, which can be whole milk or plant-based milk according to preference.

  • Flavors (Optional): Flavors such as vanilla or caramel can be added, or a sprinkle of cocoa or cinnamon on top.


Coffee to Milk Ratio:


  • The common ratio is 1:3 (one shot of espresso to three parts steamed milk).


Hot Latte Preparation:


Preparation Steps:


  1. Prepare the Espresso:

    • Grind 18-20 grams of coffee beans to a suitable fineness.

    • Place the coffee in the espresso machine filter and tamp it gently.

    • Brew a single or double shot of espresso (about 30-60 ml).

  2. Steam the Milk:

    • Use a milk steamer to steam 90-120 ml of milk until it is creamy with a light froth.

  3. Combine Espresso and Milk:

    • In a serving cup, pour the brewed espresso.

    • Slowly add the steamed milk over the espresso.

  4. Garnish (Optional):

    • You can garnish the surface with a sprinkle of cocoa or cinnamon.



Iced Latte Preparation:


Preparation Steps:


  1. Prepare the Espresso:

    • Grind 18-20 grams of coffee beans as mentioned above.

    • Brew a shot of espresso.

  2. Chill the Milk:

    • Chill 90-120 ml of milk in the refrigerator.

  3. Prepare the Ice:

    • Place some ice in a serving cup.

  4. Combine Espresso and Milk:

    • Pour the brewed espresso over the ice.

    • Add the cold milk over the espresso.

  5. Garnish (Optional):

    • You can garnish the surface with a sprinkle of cocoa or cinnamon.



Tips:


  • Froth: To achieve perfect froth, avoid overheating the milk while steaming. The ideal temperature ranges between 60-70°C (140-158°F).

  • Serving: It’s preferred to serve the latte in a glass or ceramic cup to enjoy its beautiful appearance.

Macchiato




Ingredients:


  • Coffee Beans: Arabica beans are preferred, and you can use light or medium roast beans.

  • Water: Use 30-60 ml of water to prepare the espresso.

  • Milk: A small amount of milk is used (usually about 10-20 ml).


Coffee to Milk Ratio:


  • The typical ratio of espresso to milk is about 1:1, with a very small amount of milk added for flavor.


Hot Macchiato Preparation:


Preparation Steps:


  1. Prepare the Espresso:

    • Grind 18-20 grams of coffee beans to a suitable fineness.

    • Place the coffee in the espresso machine filter and tamp it gently.

    • Brew a single or double shot of espresso (about 30-60 ml).

  2. Add the Milk:

    • Steam 10-20 ml of milk until warm with a light froth.

    • Gently add the milk over the espresso.

  3. Serving:

    • It’s preferred to serve macchiato in a small cup.

    • You can sprinkle cocoa or cinnamon on top if desired.



Iced Macchiato Preparation:


Preparation Steps:


  1. Prepare the Espresso:

    • Grind 18-20 grams of coffee beans as mentioned above.

    • Brew a shot of espresso.

  2. Prepare the Ice:

    • Place an appropriate amount of ice in a serving cup.

  3. Add the Milk:

    • Pour 10-20 ml of cold milk over the ice.

  4. Combine the Espresso:

    • Pour the brewed espresso over the ice and milk.

  5. Serving:

    • It’s preferred to serve iced macchiato in a glass cup.

    • You can add a sprinkle of cocoa or cinnamon if desired.



Tips:


  • Froth: To achieve a light froth, you can use whole milk or almond milk, as they help achieve the ideal result.

  • Serving: It’s preferred to serve macchiato hot or cold in small cups to enjoy the concentrated flavor.
